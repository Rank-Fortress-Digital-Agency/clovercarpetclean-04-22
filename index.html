Carpet Cleaning Do’s and Don’ts

If you’re like most homeowners, you probably don’t think about cleaning your carpet very often. And that’s okay – as long as you know the right way to do it! Here are some Do's and Don'ts to help you get the job done right

Do vacuum your carpets at least once a week.

This is the best way to remove dirt and dust from your carpets, and it's especially important if you have kids or pets. Be sure to use a vacuum cleaner with a good suction power, and make sure to move the cleaner around in different directions to get all the dirt.

Don't wait too long to clean spills

If you spill something on your carpet, be sure to clean it up right away. The longer you wait, the harder it will be to remove the stain. Use a damp cloth or a carpet cleaner to address the spill as soon as possible.

Do use a carpet cleaner to get deep-down dirt and stains

Carpet cleaners can be a great way to get rid of deep-down dirt and stains that are tough to remove. Be sure to follow the instructions on the bottle, and test the cleaner in an inconspicuous area before using it on your carpets.

Don't overwet your carpets when you're cleaning them

It's important to wet your carpets enough to clean them, but you don't want to overwet them. Excessive moisture can damage your carpets and create a breeding ground for mold and bacteria. Be sure to blot the area dry after cleaning it.

How often you should have your carpets cleaned

How often you should have your carpets cleaned depends on a few factors, including how often you vacuum, how many people and pets live in your home, and how dirty your carpets are. Most professionals recommend having your carpets cleaned every 6 to 12 months. But if you have a lot of traffic or pets, you may need to have them cleaned more often.
